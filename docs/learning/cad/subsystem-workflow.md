## Overview

A drivetrain isn't done when the frame is built — it needs a battery, electronics,
a bellypan to mount them on, and bumpers. This lesson applies the top-down workflow
to finishing a real drivebase, the last step before the mechanism lessons.

This lesson covers **Stage 1E** of the
[FRCDesign.org Learning Course](https://frcdesign.org/learning-course/).

## Prerequisites

- [Top-Down Design Methodology](../design-methodology/) completed.

## How to work through it

- [ ] Follow the guided project on your copied drivebase document.
- [ ] Keep weight, access, and the rules in mind as you package components.
- [ ] Verify clearances — wires, battery, and bumpers all need room.

## Unit 1 — Battery mounting

The battery is heavy and must be held securely and low for a good center of
gravity, while still being quick to swap between matches.

**Exercise 1 — Battery holder.** Model a battery holder/retention that locks the
battery in place but allows fast removal.

## Unit 2 — Electronics

Plan where the **battery, main breaker, PDH/PDP, RoboRIO, radio, and motor
controllers** go. Components need to be reachable, wires need to route cleanly, and
nothing should sit where a mechanism will land.

**Exercise 2 — Mounting electronics.** Lay out and mount the electronics board so
everything fits and is serviceable.

## Unit 3 — Bellypan

The **bellypan** is the flat plate spanning the frame that the electronics mount
to. It's pocketed to save weight while staying stiff.

**Exercise 3 — Bellypan pocketing.** Pocket the bellypan to remove material
without weakening it where it matters.

## Unit 4 — Bumpers

Bumpers protect the robot and are tightly regulated by the game rules (height,
coverage, corner protection, color).

**Exercise 4 — Bumpers.** Model bumpers that meet the rules around your frame.

**Exercise 5 — Bumper mounting.** Design a bumper mounting system that's secure but
quick to attach and remove (and to swap alliance colors).

## Review & summary

At the end you have a complete, packaged drivebase: frame, swerve modules,
battery, electronics, bellypan, and bumpers — ready to carry a competition mechanism.

## Notes

- Read the current season's bumper and electronics rules before finalizing —
  these change year to year.
- Service access is a real constraint: a perfect package you can't reach to fix is
  a bad package.
