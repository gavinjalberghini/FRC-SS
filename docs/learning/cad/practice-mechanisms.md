## Overview

By now you know the tools; this lesson is about **reps**. You'll model eight small
mechanisms that get progressively less guided, combining the structure, plate, and
power-transmission skills from the previous lessons. Don't rush — the goal is to
make the workflow automatic so the mechanism lessons feel easy.

This lesson covers **Stage 1C** of the
[FRCDesign.org Learning Course](https://frcdesign.org/learning-course/).

## Prerequisites

- [Onshape Fundamentals](../onshape-fundamentals/) and
  [Power Transmission](../power-transmission/) completed.

## How to work through it

- [ ] Do the exercises in order; each gives you less guidance than the last.
- [ ] When you get stuck, try to reason it out before asking — that's the point.
- [ ] Sanity-check motion and clearances on every model.

## The exercises

- **Exercise 1 — Flat Intake.** A simple roller intake mounted to structure.
  Practice mounting rollers and a driving motor.
- **Exercise 2 — Dead Axle Rollers.** Rollers spinning on a fixed ("dead") axle,
  with bearings inside the roller instead of on the shaft.
- **Exercise 3 — Shooter.** A flywheel shooter — your first taste of the
  [Basic Shooter](../basic-shooter/) lesson.
- **Exercise 4 — Telescoping Hook.** A two-stage telescoping mechanism; practice
  nested moving parts and clearances.
- **Exercise 5 — Flipped Gearbox.** Re-orient a gearbox to fit a tight package —
  practice flipping and re-mating a power transmission.
- **Exercise 6 — Direction Swap.** Add an idler/stage to reverse output direction
  without changing the motor.
- **Exercise 7 — Vertical Rollers.** Rollers oriented vertically (e.g., for
  centering or feeding), practicing different mounting orientations.
- **Exercise 8 — Indexer Centering.** A centering indexer that funnels game pieces
  to the middle — combines rollers, structure, and geometry.

## Notes

- These are deliberately under-specified. If two valid approaches exist, pick one
  and note why — that judgement is the skill you're building.
- Reuse the FeatureScripts (Tube Converter, Robot Shaft, Belt & Chain Gen)
  everywhere; speed comes from leaning on them.
