## Overview

A robotics subsystem is built by a handful of people working closely together
under time pressure. Small teams can move incredibly fast — or grind to a halt in
confusion and friction. The difference is rarely talent; it's how well the group
collaborates. This lesson covers what makes a small team click.

## Prerequisites

- [Communication Fundamentals](../communication-fundamentals/).

## What you'll learn

- How shared goals and clear roles prevent confusion and duplication.
- What psychological safety is and why it's the #1 driver of team performance.
- Day-to-day habits that keep a small team coordinated.

## Unit 1 — Shared goals & clear roles

- **One shared goal.** Everyone should be able to state what the team is trying to
  accomplish *right now* in one sentence. If people would answer differently,
  that's your first problem.
- **Clear roles.** Decide who owns what. Ambiguity causes two failure modes: work
  done twice, and work nobody does. Roles can be fluid on a small team, but at any
  moment each task should have **one owner**.

## Unit 2 — Psychological safety

Psychological safety is the shared belief that it's safe to **speak up, ask
questions, admit mistakes, and disagree** without being punished or embarrassed.
Research (e.g. Google's Project Aristotle) found it's the strongest predictor of
team performance.

You build it by:

- Responding to questions and "dumb ideas" with curiosity, not ridicule.
- Admitting your own mistakes first.
- Thanking people for raising problems early.
- Never letting one person dominate or belittle others.

## Unit 3 — Collaboration habits

- **Make work visible.** Use a board (see the Kanban lesson in Programming) so
  everyone sees who's doing what.
- **Sync briefly and often** rather than rarely and long (next lesson covers
  standups).
- **Decide, then commit.** Debate is good; once the group decides, everyone
  supports it — even those who argued the other way.
- **Share context, not just tasks.** People do better work when they know *why*.

## Unit 4 — Handling the load together

- Watch for teammates who are stuck or overloaded and redistribute.
- Pair up on hard problems — two people unstick faster than one.
- Celebrate small wins together; momentum is a team resource.

## Steps & acceptance criteria

- [ ] State your team's current shared goal in one sentence and check it against a
      teammate's answer.
- [ ] Define clear single owners for the tasks on a current project.
- [ ] Name two specific things you'll do to strengthen psychological safety.
- [ ] Set up or use a visible work board for your group.
