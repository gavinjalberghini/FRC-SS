## Overview

Meetings are where a team's time goes to live or die. A good meeting aligns
everyone and produces decisions; a bad one burns an hour and decides nothing. As
a team leader you'll run meetings constantly — this lesson makes them short,
focused, and worth attending.

## Prerequisites

- [Small-Team Collaboration](../small-team-collaboration/).

## What you'll learn

- How to plan a meeting that's worth holding.
- How to run a fast, useful daily standup.
- How to capture decisions and action items so they actually happen.

## Unit 1 — Before the meeting

- **Have a purpose.** If you can't say what decision or outcome the meeting is
  for, don't hold it — send a message instead.
- **Write an agenda** with topics and rough times, and share it ahead.
- **Invite only who's needed.** Bigger meetings are slower; others can read notes.

## Unit 2 — The daily standup

A standup is a short (5–15 min) sync, ideally standing up to keep it brief. Each
person answers three questions:

1. What did I get done?
2. What am I doing next?
3. What's **blocking** me?

Rules that keep it fast:

- **Status, not problem-solving.** If two people need to dig into something, they
  take it "offline" after standup.
- **Everyone participates**, briefly.
- Surface blockers so a leader can clear them.

## Unit 3 — Running the room

- **Start and end on time** — respecting time builds attendance.
- **Timebox** each topic; park tangents in a "parking lot" list.
- **Draw out quiet people** and gently rein in dominators.
- **Drive to decisions**, not just discussion.

## Unit 4 — After the meeting

End every meeting by capturing:

- **Decisions** made.
- **Action items**, each with **one owner and a due date**.

Share these where the team can see them. A decision with no owner is a wish.

## Steps & acceptance criteria

- [ ] Write an agenda with a clear purpose for an upcoming meeting.
- [ ] Facilitate a standup that stays under 15 minutes and surfaces blockers.
- [ ] Timebox a discussion and use a parking lot for tangents.
- [ ] Capture and share decisions and owned action items after a meeting.
