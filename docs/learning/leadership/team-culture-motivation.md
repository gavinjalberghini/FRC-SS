## Overview

Culture is "how we do things around here" — the unwritten norms that shape every
interaction. On a volunteer team, where nobody can be ordered to care, culture
and motivation *are* the engine. Senior leaders are the primary shapers of both.
This lesson covers how to build a culture people want to be part of and keep them
energized through a grueling season.

## Prerequisites

- All Emerging Leader and Team Leader lessons.

## What you'll learn

- What team culture is and how leaders shape it.
- What actually motivates volunteers (it isn't what most people think).
- How to recognize work and sustain energy across a long season.

## Unit 1 — Culture is set by behavior

Culture isn't a poster on the wall — it's the **worst behavior you tolerate** and
the **best behavior you celebrate**. As a leader:

- **You set norms by what you model and what you allow.** If you let a star member
  belittle others, that becomes the culture, no matter what your values say.
- **Make norms explicit** — punctuality, respect, safety, gracious
  professionalism — and hold *everyone* to them, including yourself and top
  performers.

## Unit 2 — What motivates volunteers

Research (Dan Pink's *Drive*) points to three intrinsic motivators far stronger
than rewards:

- **Autonomy** — ownership over their work and how they do it.
- **Mastery** — visibly getting better at something that matters.
- **Purpose** — understanding how their work connects to the team's mission.

So: give people real ownership, help them grow, and constantly connect daily
tasks to the bigger "why."

## Unit 3 — Recognition

- **Recognize specifically and publicly.** "Maya's wiring made inspection
  painless" beats "good job everyone."
- **Catch people doing things right**, not just things wrong.
- **Spread credit widely**; hoard it and motivation evaporates.
- Recognize **effort and growth**, not only star results — it keeps the whole
  team engaged.

## Unit 4 — Sustaining energy

A season is a marathon. Protect against burnout:

- **Watch the load** — the most committed people burn out first; make them rest.
- **Celebrate milestones**, not just the final result.
- **Build belonging** — food, traditions, inside jokes, and downtime matter.
- **Model balance** — if you grind 24/7, the team thinks they must too.

## Steps & acceptance criteria

- [ ] Name three explicit norms your team lives by and how they're reinforced.
- [ ] Identify how you give a teammate autonomy, mastery, and purpose.
- [ ] Give specific public recognition to someone this week.
- [ ] Spot a burnout risk on your team and propose a way to relieve it.
