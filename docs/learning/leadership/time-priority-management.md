## Overview

A build season is a race against a fixed deadline with never enough time. Leaders
who manage time and priorities well get the important things done; those who
don't stay busy but fall behind. This lesson is about working on the **right**
things — for yourself and for the team you lead.

## Prerequisites

- [Managing Expectations](../managing-expectations/) and [Delegation & Accountability](../delegation-accountability/).

## What you'll learn

- How to tell important work from merely urgent work.
- How to prioritize a team's task list.
- How to protect focus time and reduce thrash.

## Unit 1 — Important vs. urgent

The **Eisenhower matrix** sorts work on two axes:

- **Important & urgent** — do now (a broken drivetrain before a match).
- **Important, not urgent** — schedule it (testing, documentation, training).
  This is the quadrant teams neglect and later regret.
- **Urgent, not important** — minimize or delegate (most interruptions).
- **Neither** — drop it.

Busy people live in "urgent." Effective people protect time for "important but
not urgent."

## Unit 2 — Prioritizing the team's work

- **Rank by impact**, not by what's easy or fun. What most moves the team toward
  its goal?
- **Find the critical path** — the chain of tasks that gates everything else.
  Protect and unblock it first.
- **Limit work in progress.** A small team doing three things well beats one doing
  ten things halfway.
- **Re-prioritize regularly** — priorities shift as the season moves.

## Unit 3 — Protecting focus

- **Batch interruptions.** Constant context-switching destroys productivity;
  group questions and check-ins.
- **Protect blocks of focus time** for deep work (yours and the team's).
- **Clear blockers fast** — as a leader, an hour you spend unblocking someone can
  save the team a day.

## Unit 4 — Managing your own time

- Plan the day's top 1–3 priorities before diving in.
- Don't let your own tasks crowd out your leadership job (unblocking, deciding,
  supporting).
- Build in slack — a fully packed plan breaks at the first surprise.

## Steps & acceptance criteria

- [ ] Sort a real task list into the four Eisenhower quadrants.
- [ ] Identify the critical path on a current project and what's gating it.
- [ ] Limit your group's work-in-progress to what it can actually finish.
- [ ] Name one way you'll protect focus time this week.
