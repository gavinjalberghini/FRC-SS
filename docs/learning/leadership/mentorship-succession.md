## Overview

The final test of a leader isn't what you accomplish — it's what the team can do
*after you leave*. On a school-based team, leaders graduate every year, so
developing your replacements isn't optional; it's the job. This capstone lesson
is about mentoring others and planning for succession so the team keeps getting
stronger.

## Prerequisites

- All Emerging Leader and Team Leader lessons, plus the other Senior Leader lessons.

## What you'll learn

- The difference between doing, delegating, and developing.
- How to mentor a teammate's growth.
- How to plan succession so knowledge and leadership survive turnover.

## Unit 1 — From doing to developing

Leaders evolve through stages:

1. **Doing** — you do the work yourself.
2. **Delegating** — you hand work to others (the Delegation lesson).
3. **Developing** — you grow people so they can lead, decide, and develop others.

Senior leadership lives in stage 3. Your measure of success becomes **how capable
the people around you are**.

## Unit 2 — Mentoring

Good mentoring is mostly **teaching people to think**, not handing them answers:

- **Ask before telling.** "What have you tried? What do you think we should do?"
  builds judgment; just answering builds dependence.
- **Let them struggle productively** — and catch them before failure becomes
  costly or unsafe.
- **Give stretch assignments** with support — real responsibility is how people
  grow fastest.
- **Share the why behind your decisions** so they learn the reasoning, not just
  the rule.

## Unit 3 — Succession planning

Because leaders graduate, plan for it deliberately:

- **Identify and grow successors early** — don't wait for your final month.
- **Spread knowledge**, don't hoard it — no single point of failure (including
  you). Pair and cross-train.
- **Document** the things only you know — processes, contacts, hard-won lessons.
  (Every lesson's "Notes" placeholder in this playbook is a place to do exactly
  that.)
- **Hand off gradually** — let successors lead while you can still coach, not all
  at once on your way out.

## Unit 4 — Leave it better

- Capture lessons learned so next year doesn't repeat your mistakes.
- Strengthen the systems (training, norms, docs), not just this year's results.
- Measure yourself by the team's **trajectory**, not your personal highlight reel.

## Steps & acceptance criteria

- [ ] Identify where you personally sit on the doing → delegating → developing
      ladder.
- [ ] Mentor a teammate by asking questions instead of giving the answer.
- [ ] Name your potential successor(s) and a plan to grow them this season.
- [ ] Document one piece of "only I know this" knowledge for the team.
