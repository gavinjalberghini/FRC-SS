## Overview

Most frustration on a team comes from a gap between what people *expected* and
what actually happened. Managing expectations is the skill of closing that gap up
front and keeping it closed — making honest commitments, saying no when needed,
and communicating changes before they become surprises. On a deadline-driven
robotics team, this skill is gold.

## Prerequisites

- [Communication Fundamentals](../communication-fundamentals/) and [Delegation & Accountability](../delegation-accountability/).

## What you'll learn

- How to make realistic commitments instead of optimistic ones.
- How to manage scope, time, and the people depending on you.
- How to deliver bad news (delays, cuts) early and well.

## Unit 1 — Make realistic commitments

- **Estimate honestly, then add buffer.** People are chronically optimistic;
  things take longer than you think. Pad for the unknown.
- **Under-promise, over-deliver** beats the reverse every time.
- **Make commitments concrete:** what, by when, to what quality. "Soon" and
  "mostly done" are not commitments.

## Unit 2 — Scope, time, quality: pick your trade-offs

You usually can't max all three. When pressure hits, decide *with your
stakeholders* what gives:

- **Cut scope** — do less, but do it well (often the best choice).
- **Move the deadline** — if it's movable.
- **Lower quality** — rarely acceptable for safety-critical robot work.

Naming the trade-off out loud turns a vague panic into a clear decision.

## Unit 3 — Manage your stakeholders

A **stakeholder** is anyone depending on your work — other subsystems, drive
team, mentors, sponsors. For each:

- Know **what they actually need** and by when.
- Keep them **informed proactively**, especially when something changes.
- Distinguish **needs from wants** and negotiate scope honestly.

## Unit 4 — Deliver bad news early

When something is going to slip or get cut:

- **Say it as soon as you know** — early bad news is a problem to solve; late bad
  news is a crisis.
- Bring the **impact and your proposed plan**, not just the problem.
- **Don't hide or hope.** Surprises destroy trust faster than delays.

## Steps & acceptance criteria

- [ ] Turn an optimistic estimate into a realistic, buffered commitment.
- [ ] Name the scope/time/quality trade-off for a project under pressure.
- [ ] Identify the stakeholders for your work and what each needs.
- [ ] Practice delivering a delay early, with impact and a proposed plan.
