## Overview

Almost every team problem is, at its root, a communication problem. Before you
can delegate, resolve conflict, or set expectations, you have to be able to
listen well and say what you mean clearly. This lesson covers the communication
fundamentals every leader relies on daily.

## Prerequisites

- [What Leadership Really Is](../what-leadership-is/).

## What you'll learn

- How to listen actively so people feel heard.
- How to deliver a message that is clear *and* kind.
- How to adapt your communication to the person and the channel.

## Unit 1 — Active listening

Listening is the most underrated leadership skill. Active listening means:

- **Give full attention** — put the phone down, face the person.
- **Don't interrupt** to insert your solution; let them finish.
- **Reflect back** what you heard ("So what I'm hearing is…") to confirm you got
  it right.
- **Ask open questions** ("What would help?") instead of leading ones.

People who feel heard are far more willing to follow, compromise, and contribute.

## Unit 2 — Clear and kind messaging

- **Lead with the point.** Say the main thing first, then the detail. Busy
  teammates shouldn't have to dig for it.
- **Be specific.** "Can you finish the intake bracket by Thursday?" beats "we
  should work on the intake."
- **Separate the behavior from the person.** Talk about what happened, not who
  someone *is*.
- **Kind ≠ soft.** You can be direct and respectful at the same time. Avoiding a
  hard message isn't kindness — it's avoidance.

## Unit 3 — Know your audience and channel

- Adapt to the **person**: a new member needs more context than a veteran.
- Adapt to the **channel**: quick coordination → chat; nuanced or emotional
  topics → in person or voice, never a wall of text.
- **Public praise, private correction.** Recognize people in front of the group;
  give critical feedback one-on-one.

## Unit 4 — Closing the loop

Communication isn't done when you've spoken — it's done when the other person
**understands and agrees on next steps**. Confirm who does what by when.

## Steps & acceptance criteria

- [ ] Demonstrate active listening in a real conversation (attention, no
      interrupting, reflecting back).
- [ ] Rewrite a vague request into a clear, specific one.
- [ ] Choose the right channel for three different messages.
- [ ] End a coordination conversation by confirming next steps and owners.
