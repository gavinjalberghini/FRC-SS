## Overview

Most people think leadership is a title — captain, lead, president. It isn't.
Leadership is **influence**: the ability to help a group move toward a shared
goal. The best leaders on a team are often the people without any title at all,
because teammates trust them. This first lesson reframes what leadership is so
the rest of the curriculum makes sense.

## Prerequisites

- None. This is the starting point for everyone.

## What you'll learn

- The difference between authority (a title) and influence (trust).
- What "servant leadership" and "leading by example" mean in practice.
- The core traits teammates actually follow.

## Unit 1 — Authority vs. influence

- **Authority** is given to you by a position. People do what you say because they
  have to.
- **Influence** is earned through trust, competence, and care. People follow you
  because they want to.

On a volunteer team like a robotics team, authority is weak — nobody is paid, and
people can disengage. **Influence is everything.** A title without trust is an
empty chair.

## Unit 2 — Leading by example

You set the standard by what you *do*, not what you say:

- Show up on time and prepared.
- Do the unglamorous work (clean-up, documentation) without being asked.
- Stay calm under pressure — your team mirrors your energy.
- Admit mistakes openly; it makes it safe for others to do the same.

## Unit 3 — Serve the team

**Servant leadership** flips the pyramid: your job is to make the team
successful, not to make yourself look good. Practically, that means removing
roadblocks, getting people what they need, giving credit away, and taking
responsibility when things go wrong.

## Unit 4 — Traits people follow

- **Competence** — you know your stuff (or you're honestly working to).
- **Consistency** — people know what to expect from you.
- **Care** — you genuinely want teammates to succeed.
- **Composure** — you keep your head when things get stressful.

## Steps & acceptance criteria

- [ ] Explain the difference between authority and influence with an example from
      your team.
- [ ] Name two ways you can lead by example in your role this week.
- [ ] Identify a person on your team who leads without a title and describe how.
- [ ] Reflect on which of the four traits is your strongest and which you'll grow.
