## Overview

As the printing lead you keep every printer healthy and producing legal parts on
schedule. This lesson covers maintenance, calibration, and running several printers
as a small print farm during build season.

## Prerequisites

- [Engineering Materials & Advanced Printing](../engineering-materials/) completed.

## Unit 1 — Routine maintenance

- **Clean the build plates** regularly and replace them when worn.
- **Nozzles** — inspect for wear (especially after abrasive filament) and replace;
  keep the right nozzle on the right printer.
- **Lubrication** — grease the lead screws/rails per Bambu's schedule; keep linear
  rails clean.
- **Belts** — check tension; loose belts cause layer shifts and ringing.
- **Hotend & extruder** — clear debris, check for clogs, and keep the path clean.
- Keep a **maintenance log** per printer.

## Unit 2 — Calibration

- Run Bambu's **auto calibration** (bed level, flow, resonance/vibration) after
  maintenance, nozzle changes, or when quality drifts.
- Calibrate **flow rate and pressure advance** per filament for best quality.
- Verify dimensional accuracy with a **test coupon** when starting a new material.

## Unit 3 — Firmware & software

- Keep printer **firmware** and **Bambu Studio** updated, but avoid updating
  mid-competition without testing.
- Maintain a shared library of **validated print profiles** so any operator gets
  consistent results.

## Unit 4 — Running a print farm

- **Queue and prioritize** jobs during build season; structural/long prints get
  scheduled deliberately.
- Track **filament inventory** (and keep engineering filament dry) so you don't run
  out mid-print.
- Assign printers to materials (e.g., one dedicated to abrasive CF) to avoid
  cross-contamination and nozzle damage.
- Label parts and track who needs what; monitor remotely via camera/app.

## Unit 5 — FRC rules & competition-legal parts

- The **FRC game manual** sets rules on materials and where 3D-printed parts may be
  used (e.g., COTS vs. fabricated, allowed plastics, structural limits). **Read the
  current season's rules.**
- Some applications are restricted; confirm a printed part is legal **before** the
  team relies on it.
- When this curriculum and the manual disagree, **the manual wins.**

## Steps & acceptance criteria

- [ ] Perform routine maintenance on a printer and log it.
- [ ] Run calibration and verify accuracy with a test coupon.
- [ ] Set up a validated, shared print profile.
- [ ] Manage a multi-printer queue and filament inventory through a build sprint.
- [ ] Confirm a printed part is competition-legal against the current manual.
