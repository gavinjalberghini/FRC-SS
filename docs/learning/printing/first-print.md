## Overview

Time to make a part. This lesson walks through everything from loading filament to
pulling a finished part off the plate on a Bambu printer.

## Prerequisites

- [Slicing in Bambu Studio](../slicing-bambu-studio/) completed.

## Unit 1 — Load filament

- **Direct spool:** mount the spool, feed filament into the extruder, and use
  **Load filament** on the screen; wait until clean plastic of the right color
  extrudes.
- **AMS:** insert the spool into a slot; the AMS reads Bambu RFID automatically or
  prompts you to set the material. Assign the slot to the right filament in the
  slice.

## Unit 2 — Prepare the build plate

- Choose the correct **build plate** for the material (e.g., textured PEI for
  PLA/PETG) and make sure it matches the slice.
- The plate must be **clean** — oils from fingerprints ruin adhesion. Wash with
  dish soap and water or wipe with IPA, and handle by the edges.
- Seat the plate fully on the bed, aligned to the locating features.

## Unit 3 — Calibration

- Bambu printers run **automatic calibration** at the start: bed leveling, and on
  capable models **flow-rate / resonance** calibration.
- For a new filament, let it run calibration; for repeats you can skip it to save
  time.

## Unit 4 — Start and monitor

- Confirm the **filament, plate, and printer** in the slice match reality, then
  start the print.
- **Watch the first layer** — it sets the whole print. It should lay down evenly
  and stick. If it's not adhering or the nozzle is digging in, stop and fix it.
- Monitor remotely with the **camera / Bambu Handy app**; you don't have to stand
  there, but check in periodically.

## Unit 5 — Finish

- Wait for the **bed to cool** — most parts pop off a flexible plate on their own
  once cool.
- Remove the part (next lesson), then **return the plate and clean up**.

## Steps & acceptance criteria

- [ ] Load a filament directly and via the AMS.
- [ ] Select and clean the correct build plate.
- [ ] Start a print and confirm a good first layer.
- [ ] Monitor the print and remove a finished part safely.

## Notes

- A clean plate fixes 90% of first-layer adhesion problems. When in doubt, wash it.
- Never restart a "failed-looking" print by force — diagnose first (see the
  troubleshooting lesson).
