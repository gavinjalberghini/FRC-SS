## Overview

Before you run a printer, you need to understand what it's doing and how to stay
safe around it. This lesson covers how fused-deposition modeling (FDM) works, the
parts of our Bambu Lab printers, and the safety habits every operator follows.
**You don't run a printer unsupervised until a mentor signs off this lesson.**

## Prerequisites

- None. This is the first lesson for every 3D-printing team member.

## Unit 1 — How FDM printing works

- An FDM printer melts plastic **filament** and lays it down in thin **layers**
  that fuse together to build a part from the bottom up.
- A digital model (an STL or 3MF file) is sliced into layers and toolpaths by a
  **slicer** (we use Bambu Studio), which produces the instructions the printer
  runs.
- Because parts are built in layers, they are **weakest between layers** — this
  drives how you orient parts for strength (covered later).

## Unit 2 — The Bambu printer lineup

- **X1 / X1-Carbon (X1C)** — enclosed, fastest, supports high-temp and abrasive
  materials; lidar and a camera for monitoring.
- **P1S** — enclosed, similar core to the X1C with fewer sensors; a great workhorse.
- **A1 / A1 mini** — open-frame, bed-slinger printers; excellent for PLA/PETG, not
  suited to high-temp materials like ABS that need an enclosure.
- **AMS / AMS lite** — the Automatic Material System that holds multiple spools for
  multi-color and multi-material printing.

## Unit 3 — Printer anatomy

- **Nozzle / hotend** — melts and extrudes filament; runs 200–300 °C.
- **Heated bed / build plate** — the removable, often textured plate parts stick to.
- **Extruder & toolhead** — pushes filament and moves in X/Y (and Z for height).
- **AMS** — feeds and switches filament automatically.
- **Touchscreen / Bambu Handy app** — start, pause, and monitor prints.

## Unit 4 — Safety

- **Hot surfaces.** The nozzle and bed burn skin instantly. Never reach into a
  running printer; let parts and the nozzle cool before touching.
- **Fumes & ventilation.** PLA/PETG are low-odor, but **ABS/ASA and nylon emit
  fumes** — run them in an enclosed printer in a ventilated area.
- **Moving parts.** Keep hair, sleeves, and fingers clear of the toolhead.
- **Tools.** Removing parts uses scrapers and flush cutters — cut and scrape
  **away** from your body and hands.
- **Electrical & fire.** Don't leave the very first layer unattended; know where
  the printer's power switch and the shop fire extinguisher are.

## Steps & acceptance criteria

- [ ] Explain how FDM builds a part and why parts are weakest between layers.
- [ ] Identify each Bambu printer in the shop and whether it's enclosed.
- [ ] Point to the nozzle, bed, extruder, and AMS on a real printer.
- [ ] State which materials require an enclosure and ventilation.
- [ ] Earn your mentor's 3D-printing safety sign-off.
