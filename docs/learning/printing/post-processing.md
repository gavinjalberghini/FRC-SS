## Overview

A print isn't done when the printer stops. This lesson covers getting the part off
the plate, removing supports, and basic finishing — all without breaking the part
or hurting yourself.

## Prerequisites

- [Running Your First Print](../first-print/) completed.

## Unit 1 — Removing the part from the plate

- **Let it cool first.** On a flexible plate, most parts release on their own as
  the plate cools — flex the plate and they pop off.
- If a part is stuck, flex the plate over an edge; use a scraper **only** as a last
  resort, pushing **away** from your hand.
- Never gouge the plate with a metal scraper — it damages the surface and ruins
  future adhesion.

## Unit 2 — Removing supports

- Supports are meant to **snap off**. Start at an edge with flush cutters or
  needle-nose pliers and work them free.
- A **brim** peels off; clean its edge with a deburring tool or knife.
- For fine or internal supports, take your time — forcing them can crack the part.

## Unit 3 — Finishing

- **Deburr** edges and remove stringing/zits with flush cutters or a deburring tool.
- **Sanding** (wet, increasing grits) smooths visible surfaces; PLA/PETG sand fine.
- Clean out **holes and threads** that will take fasteners or heat-set inserts;
  a quick pass with a drill bit or tap by hand restores the size.
- Inspect for layer separation or under-extrusion before the part goes on the robot.

## Unit 4 — Tool & cleanup safety

- Cut and scrape **away** from your body; flush cutters launch bits of plastic —
  wear eye protection.
- Return the build plate, recycle scrap supports, and leave the area clean.

## Steps & acceptance criteria

- [ ] Remove a part from the plate without damaging the part or plate.
- [ ] Cleanly remove supports and a brim.
- [ ] Deburr and clean fastener holes on a printed part.
- [ ] Inspect a part and decide whether it's good enough to use.
