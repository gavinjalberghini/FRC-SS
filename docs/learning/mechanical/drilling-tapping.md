## Overview

Most parts get joined through holes — clearance holes for bolts to pass through,
and tapped (threaded) holes for bolts to thread into. Drilling accurate holes and
cutting clean threads are essential fabrication skills. This lesson covers the
drill press and the tapping process.

## Prerequisites

- [Measurement & Layout](../measurement-layout/), [Fasteners & Hardware](../fasteners-hardware/), and [Drills & Drivers](../drills-drivers/).

## What you'll learn

- Safe, accurate drill press operation.
- The difference between a clearance hole and a tap (threaded) hole.
- How to choose the correct drill bit for a bolt or a tap.
- How to cut clean threads with a tap.

## Unit 1 — The drill press

The drill press holds the bit perpendicular and lets you control depth — far more
accurate than a hand drill for precise holes.

- **Clamp the work** to the table or in a drill vise. A bit that grabs an
  unclamped part will spin it into a dangerous "helicopter" — this is why you
  always clamp and **never wear gloves**.
- **Set the speed** with the belt/pulley: large bits and metal run **slower**,
  small bits run faster.
- **Center-punch** the hole location so the bit starts on the mark.
- Bring the bit down **smoothly**, ease off as it breaks through, and **peck**
  (back out to clear chips) on deeper holes. Use cutting fluid on metal.

## Unit 2 — Clearance vs. tapped holes

- A **clearance hole** is slightly **larger** than the bolt so it passes through
  freely (e.g. a clearance hole for a #10 screw).
- A **tapped hole** is threaded so a bolt screws directly into the part. It must
  be drilled with the correct **tap drill** size first — smaller than the bolt, so
  there's material for the threads.

Use a tap/drill chart: each thread size (e.g. #10-32, 1/4-20) has a specific tap
drill diameter and a clearance drill diameter.

## Unit 3 — Tapping threads

1. Drill the hole with the **correct tap drill** size, then deburr.
2. Choose the matching **tap** (e.g. a #10-32 tap) and a **tap handle**.
3. Start the tap **square** to the hole. A few drops of cutting fluid help.
4. Turn in about a **half turn**, then **back off a quarter turn** to break the
   chip. Repeat. This rhythm keeps the tap from binding and snapping.
5. Keep it straight and don't force it — taps are hard and **brittle**, and a
   snapped tap in a hole is very hard to remove.

## Unit 4 — Finishing

- **Deburr both sides** of every hole so parts seat flat and threads start clean.
- Test-fit the bolt; it should pass (clearance) or thread smoothly (tapped)
  without forcing.

## Steps & acceptance criteria

- [ ] Set up and run the drill press safely (clamped work, correct speed, no
      gloves).
- [ ] Choose the correct clearance drill and tap drill for a given bolt size.
- [ ] Drill an accurate, deburred hole on a punched mark.
- [ ] Cut clean #10-32 (or assigned size) threads with a tap without breaking it.
