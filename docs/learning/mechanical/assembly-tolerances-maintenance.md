## Overview

A robot is more than a pile of well-made parts — it's an assembly that has to fit
together, move freely, and survive the abuse of competition. This capstone lesson
covers building to tolerance, aligning mechanisms, and the maintenance habits
that keep a robot running all season.

## Prerequisites

- All of Level 1 and Level 2, and [Power Transmission & Drivetrains](../power-transmission-drivetrains/).

## What you'll learn

- What tolerance and fit mean in assembly.
- How to align and square a mechanism so it moves freely.
- How to design and build for serviceability.
- A maintenance routine for competition reliability.

## Unit 1 — Tolerance & fit

- A **tolerance** is how much a dimension is allowed to vary (e.g. 2.000 ± 0.005
  in). Tighter tolerances cost more time — only call for them where they matter
  (bearing bores, gear centers).
- **Clearance fit** parts slide together; **press/interference fit** parts (like a
  bearing in a bore) are slightly larger and pressed in.
- If two parts don't fit, **measure to find which is out** rather than forcing
  them.

## Unit 2 — Alignment & squareness

- Assemble against **reference faces** and check with a square — a frame that
  isn't square will never drive straight.
- Shafts must be **parallel** and gears/sprockets **coplanar**, or they bind and
  wear.
- Spin and cycle every mechanism **by hand** before powering it; free motion now
  prevents burned motors later.

## Unit 3 — Build for serviceability

- Leave **tool access** to every fastener you might need to remove.
- Use **bolts (not rivets)** where you'll need to adjust or replace parts.
- **Label and standardize** fasteners so repairs are fast.
- Route and secure wires/hose so they don't get pinched during service (coordinate
  with electrical).

## Unit 4 — Maintenance routine

- **Before/after each match or practice block:** check for loose fasteners,
  chain/belt tension, bearing play, and damage.
- Keep a **checklist** and a small **pit toolkit** stocked.
- **Re-torque** critical fasteners and apply thread locker where things back out.
- Log recurring failures so the design can be improved.

## Steps & acceptance criteria

- [ ] Explain tolerance, clearance fit, and press fit with examples.
- [ ] Square and align a mechanism and confirm it moves freely by hand.
- [ ] Critique a sample assembly for serviceability and improve it.
- [ ] Produce a pre-match mechanical inspection checklist.
