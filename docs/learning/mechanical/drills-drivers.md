## Overview

The cordless drill and impact driver are the most-used power tools on the team.
They look similar but do different jobs, and using the wrong one (or the wrong
settings) strips fasteners and breaks bits. This lesson covers how each works,
how to choose and seat bits, and the technique for clean holes and properly
driven fasteners.

## Prerequisites

- [Shop Safety & PPE](../shop-safety/) and [Hand Tools](../hand-tools/).

## What you'll learn

- The difference between a drill/driver and an impact driver.
- How the chuck, clutch, speed, and direction controls work.
- How to select drill bits and driver bits.
- Technique for drilling holes and driving fasteners without damage.

## Unit 1 — Drill/driver vs. impact driver

- **Drill/driver** — has a **keyless chuck** that grips round drill bits and a
  **clutch** (the numbered collar) that slips at a set torque to avoid
  overdriving. Use it for **drilling holes** and for careful, torque-controlled
  driving.
- **Impact driver** — has a **1/4 in hex collet** (hex bits only) and delivers
  rotational **hammer "impacts"** for high torque in a small body. Use it for
  **driving lots of screws** or stubborn fasteners — but it's easy to overdrive
  and strip with, so it's usually *not* for delicate or precision work.

## Unit 2 — Controls

- **Chuck** — loosen/tighten by hand (keyless). Seat the bit fully and centered,
  then tighten firmly so it doesn't slip.
- **Direction (FWD/REV)** — forward to drive/drill, reverse to remove.
- **Clutch collar** — lower numbers slip sooner (less torque, good for small
  fasteners); the drill symbol locks out the clutch for drilling.
- **Speed (gear) selector** — low gear = more torque/less speed (driving, big
  bits); high gear = more speed (small drill bits).

## Unit 3 — Bits

- **Twist drill bits** for holes — match the bit to the hole size (and to the tap
  drill size when you'll thread the hole; see Drilling & Tapping). Use bits rated
  for metal.
- **Driver bits** — match the **type and size** exactly (e.g. a 3/32 hex bit for a
  #10 SHCS). The wrong size cams out and strips the head.
- **Brad-point / step / countersink** bits for specific jobs.

## Unit 4 — Technique

- **Center-punch** the hole location first so the bit doesn't wander.
- Start slow to establish the hole, then bring up speed; let the tool cut without
  forcing it.
- Keep the bit **perpendicular** to the surface (or to the drawing's intent).
- For metal, a drop of **cutting fluid** keeps the bit cool and cutting; back the
  bit out to clear chips.
- When driving, start the clutch low and increase until the fastener seats flush —
  don't overdrive into aluminum.

## Steps & acceptance criteria

- [ ] Explain when to use a drill/driver vs. an impact driver.
- [ ] Set the clutch, speed gear, and direction correctly for a given task.
- [ ] Seat a drill bit and a driver bit correctly and centered.
- [ ] Drill a clean, perpendicular hole in aluminum and drive a screw flush
      without stripping it.
