## Overview

After cutting and drilling, parts need finishing: knocking down sharp edges,
smoothing cuts, and removing small amounts of material to fit. Grinders, sanders,
and rotary tools do this fast — and because they remove material quickly and
throw sparks and dust, they demand respect. This lesson covers their safe
operation.

## Prerequisites

- [Shop Safety & PPE](../shop-safety/) and [Cutting Materials](../cutting-materials/).

## What you'll learn

- Safe operation of the belt/disc sander, angle grinder, and rotary tool.
- Which tool to use for deburring, finishing, and shaping.
- The PPE and hazards specific to abrasive tools.

## Unit 1 — Belt & disc sander

The bench belt/disc sander is the safest, most-used finishing tool: great for
squaring ends, smoothing edges, and removing small amounts of material.

- Work on the **down-running** side of the disc/belt so it pushes the part into
  the table, not up at you.
- Use the **table/fence** for support and to hold an angle.
- Move the part and use light pressure; pressing hard overheats aluminum and
  loads the belt. Keep fingers back from the abrasive.

## Unit 2 — Angle grinder

A handheld angle grinder removes material fast with a cutoff or grinding wheel —
powerful and higher-risk.

- **Two hands, firm stance.** Secure the workpiece in a vise.
- Let the wheel reach full speed before contact; **never** grind on the side of a
  cutoff wheel (it can shatter).
- Mind **kickback** and the **spark stream** — point it away from people,
  flammables, and batteries.
- Face shield over safety glasses, hearing protection, and no gloves near a
  pinch/grab risk per your shop's rule.

## Unit 3 — Rotary tools (Dremel)

Small rotary tools take cutoff discs, sanding drums, and burrs for detail work,
slots, and deburring tight spots.

- Secure small parts; the bit can grab and fling them.
- Light touch and let the speed do the work; cutoff discs are fragile.
- Eye protection always — they throw fine debris.

## Unit 4 — Choosing the tool

- **Deburring an edge or hole:** deburring tool, file, or a quick pass on the
  sander.
- **Squaring/smoothing a cut:** belt/disc sander.
- **Fast bulk removal or rough cutoff:** angle grinder.
- **Detail/tight spots:** rotary tool.

## Steps & acceptance criteria

- [ ] Square and smooth a cut end on the belt/disc sander using the table.
- [ ] State the angle grinder safety rules (full speed first, no side grinding,
      spark direction, secure work).
- [ ] Use a rotary tool to deburr a tight feature safely.
- [ ] Select the appropriate finishing tool for three sample tasks.
