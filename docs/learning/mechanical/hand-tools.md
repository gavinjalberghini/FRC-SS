## Overview

Before you pick up a power tool, you need to be fluent with hand tools. They're
safer, more precise for small jobs, and often faster than setting up a machine.
This lesson covers the manual tools every mechanical member uses daily and the
technique to use them well.

## Prerequisites

- All of Level 1 (Safety, Measurement, Fasteners, Materials).

## What you'll learn

- The common hand tools and what each is for.
- Correct technique to avoid stripping, slipping, and injury.
- How to make a clean cut and a smooth edge by hand.

## Unit 1 — Driving fasteners

- **Hex (Allen) keys** — drive socket-head screws (the FRC standard). Use the
  correct size, seat it fully, and keep the key square to the screw. L-keys and
  ball-end keys each have a place; ball-ends are for angle access only, not final
  torque.
- **Screwdrivers** — match the tip type and size (Phillips, flat, Torx). A
  too-small tip strips the head.
- **Wrenches & sockets** — open-end, box-end, and ratchets. Pull toward you when
  you can, and use the right size so you don't round the bolt. Nut drivers speed
  up small hex nuts.

## Unit 2 — Cutting & shaping by hand

- **Hacksaw** — cuts metal stock with a fine-tooth blade. Mount the blade with
  teeth pointing **forward** (cuts on the push stroke), use long strokes, and let
  the saw do the work.
- **Files** — remove material and smooth edges. Cut on the **push** stroke only;
  lift on the return. A mill file for flats, a round/half-round for curves.
- **Deburring tool** — removes the sharp burr left after cutting or drilling so
  parts fit and won't cut you.

## Unit 3 — Holding & helpers

- **Clamps and a vise** hold work so both your hands are free and the part can't
  move. Never hold small stock with your fingers while cutting or drilling.
- **Center punch** marks and dimples a hole location so a drill won't wander.
- **Tape measure, combination square, and calipers** (from the measurement
  lesson) live in your kit too.

## Steps & acceptance criteria

- [ ] Select the correct hex key/driver for a fastener and drive it without
      stripping.
- [ ] Cut a piece of stock square with a hacksaw.
- [ ] Deburr and file a cut edge smooth and flat.
- [ ] Set up a part in a vise/clamp safely before working on it.
