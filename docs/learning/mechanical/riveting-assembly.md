## Overview

Once parts are cut, drilled, and finished, you join them. FRC uses two main
fastened joints: **rivets** for permanent, lightweight connections (like gussets
to box tube) and **bolts** for joints that must be adjustable or serviceable.
This lesson covers riveting technique and good bolted-assembly practice.

## Prerequisites

- [Fasteners & Hardware](../fasteners-hardware/) and [Drilling & Tapping](../drilling-tapping/).

## What you'll learn

- How a pop (blind) rivet works and how to install one.
- How to use a hand and/or pneumatic rivet tool.
- When to rivet vs. bolt, and how to make strong, serviceable bolted joints.

## Unit 1 — How a pop rivet works

A **blind rivet** installs from one side: you insert the rivet body through
aligned holes, and the rivet tool pulls the **mandrel**, which expands the back of
the rivet and then **snaps off**, clamping the parts together permanently.

- Match the **rivet diameter** to the hole (e.g. **1/8 in rivets in 1/8 in holes**
  — a common FRC standard) and the **grip range** to the combined material
  thickness.
- Rivets are light and fast but **permanent** — to undo one you drill it out.

## Unit 2 — Riveting technique

1. Deburr and align the holes; clamp the parts so they can't shift.
2. Load the rivet into the tool's nosepiece (correct size).
3. Insert the rivet fully into the hole, hold the tool **square** to the surface.
4. Squeeze the handle (or trigger a pneumatic riveter) until the mandrel snaps.
5. Check the back side seated flat; collect the spent mandrels.

A **pneumatic rivet tool** makes this effortless for many rivets — same rules,
just faster; keep fingers clear of the nose.

## Unit 3 — Bolted assembly best practices

- Use bolts where you need to **adjust, tension, or service** a joint later.
- Use a **clearance hole** on the pass-through part and a **tapped hole** or a
  **nylock nut** on the other side.
- Don't overtighten into aluminum; use thread locker where the build calls for it.
- Leave access for a tool — a bolt you can't reach is a bolt you can't service.
