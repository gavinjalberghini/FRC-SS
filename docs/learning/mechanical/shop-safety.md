## Overview

Everything the mechanical team does starts with safety. Shop tools cut, grind,
and spin fast enough to cause permanent injury in a fraction of a second. This
lesson covers the habits and protective equipment that keep you and your
teammates safe. **You cannot use any machine until a mentor has signed off your
shop-safety training** — this lesson is that gate.

## Prerequisites

- None. This is the first lesson for every mechanical team member.

## What you'll learn

- The personal protective equipment (PPE) required in the shop.
- Safe behavior and awareness around running machines.
- What to do when something goes wrong.

## Unit 1 — Personal protective equipment

- **Safety glasses, always.** ANSI-rated eye protection is worn by everyone in
  the shop, not just the person running a tool. Chips fly.
- **No loose anything.** Tie back long hair, remove hoodie strings, rings, and
  dangling jewelry, and roll up loose sleeves. Anything that can get caught will.
- **Closed-toe shoes** at all times.
- **Hearing protection** for loud tools (chop saw, grinder).
- **Gloves: it depends.** Gloves protect against sharp edges when handling stock,
  but you **never** wear gloves at a rotating tool (drill press, lathe, bandsaw,
  mill) — they can catch and pull your hand in.

## Unit 2 — Behavior around machines

- **One operator per tool.** The person running the machine is in charge of it.
- **Respect the danger zone.** Keep hands, hair, and clothing clear of any
  cutting or spinning part. Use push sticks and clamps, not fingers.
- **Don't startle an operator.** Wait until a cut is finished to talk to someone
  using a tool.
- **Clean as you go.** Chips and clutter cause slips and hide hazards.
- **Know where the stops are** — every machine's off switch and the shop's main
  power cutoff.

## Unit 3 — When something goes wrong

- **Stop the tool first**, then assess.
- Know the location of the **first-aid kit** and **fire extinguisher**.
- Report every injury, near-miss, and broken tool to a mentor immediately.

## Steps & acceptance criteria

- [ ] Demonstrate correct PPE before entering the work area.
- [ ] Explain when gloves are and are not allowed, and why.
- [ ] Locate the off switch on each machine and the shop's main cutoff.
- [ ] Locate the first-aid kit and fire extinguisher.
- [ ] Earn your mentor's shop-safety sign-off.
