This guide is the **authoritative reference** for how robot design and build
decisions get made on the team. The principle is simple: decisions are made as
close to the work as possible, and only escalate when they need to. Once a decision
is made through this process, **everyone is expected to respect it** — see the
[Team Contract]({{ '/contract/' | relative_url }}).

## Where decisions are made day to day

Most decisions never need a vote. They are made by the right person at the right
level — the same role tiers described in the [Org Chart]({{ '/org-chart/' | relative_url }}).

### Within your tasking — you decide

- If a decision lives **inside the task you've been given**, you make it. Veterans
  are expected to work independently and own the calls within their scope.
- Document what you decided and why, and flag anything that affects other people's
  tasks before it becomes a problem.

### Across tasks — leads facilitate

- **Leads assist** with decisions that reach beyond a single task and **facilitate
  the distribution of work** — making sure the right tasks go to the right people
  and that dependencies between trades line up.
- Bring a decision to your lead when it grows past your own task or you're blocked.

### Strategy & priority — mentors drive

- **Mentors drive the overall strategy** of task execution and **priority** — what
  the team works on, and in what order, to meet the season's goals.

## Making a formal decision

When a decision is significant, contested, or can't be resolved at the levels
above, it goes through the team's formal process, in order:

1. **The student leads convene and vote.** The
   [Student Leadership Team]({{ '/org-chart/' | relative_url }}) — the Leads, who
   serve as co-captains — discusses the options and votes.
2. **The adults have the final say.** The team is student-driven, but mentors hold
   final authority. The leads' vote informs that call; it does not override it.
3. **The head teacher breaks a tie.** If the adults cannot reach agreement, the
   **head teacher** makes the final decision.

## Respecting the outcome

- It is the **responsibility of every member** — student and mentor alike — to
  respect and support decisions made through this process, **even when they argued
  for something else**.
- Raise your concerns through your lead *before* a decision is made. Once it's made,
  get behind it so the team can move forward together.
- This expectation is part of the [Team Contract]({{ '/contract/' | relative_url }}).
