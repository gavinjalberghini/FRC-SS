## Vision

We want to be a team that performs well not once, but consistently â and we
believe consistency is built by people, not trophies. Our vision rests on three
commitments:

### Consistent performance through mentorship and shared learning

We want to compete consistently and qualify for championship-level events. The
way we get there is by instilling **mentorship and shared learning** in every
student. We aren't trying to create one-time all-stars; we're developing
individuals who can impart their knowledge and impact to the next generation.
That takes **foresight** and a **commitment to each other** â investing in
teammates today so the team is stronger long after any one of us has moved on.

- Build a culture where students teach students and knowledge is passed down, not
  lost to graduation.
- Develop depth at every role so the team never depends on a single person.
- Plan ahead â for the next match, the next season, and the next generation.

### An increased impact on our community

We want to grow our impact beyond our own team. The goal is to **share our
opportunities and excitement with others** so that the whole STEM community
around us is elevated.

- Mentor and support rookie and nearby teams.
- Share designs, code, and lessons learned openly.
- Bring the excitement of STEM to our schools and broader community.

### A student-led team

We want to remain **student-led**, with mentors coaching rather than directing.
Students should feel empowered to take genuine **ownership and responsibility**
for real-world challenges and systems â and to grow into leaders through that
responsibility.

- Put students in charge of real decisions, budgets, and subsystems.
- Use mentors as coaches and guides, not managers.
- Sustain a strong pipeline of new members and emerging leaders year over year.
